FAQs

Something on my finance agreement doesn't look right. What should I do?

Get in touch with the dealership that arranged your finance. They have access to your original paperwork and can liaise with us to correct any errors. In most cases, updates are reflected on your account within five working days. If you'd rather speak to us directly, our customer services team is happy to help on 0345 786 4321.

I have an issue with my vehicle. Who do I contact?

Your first point of contact should always be the supplying dealer, as they are responsible for the condition of the vehicle at the point of sale. If you're unable to reach a resolution, come back to us — under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 you may have additional protections as the finance provider, and we'll work with you and the dealer to find a fair outcome.

How do I make a complaint about my agreement or the service I've received?

We're sorry to hear something hasn't met your expectations. You can raise a complaint by calling 0345 786 4321, writing to us at Azadi Financial Services, Imaginary Road, Birmingham B28 0HB, or emailing the details to our customer services team. We aim to acknowledge every complaint within two working days and to provide a full response within eight weeks. If you remain dissatisfied, you have the right to refer the matter to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

Why is my outstanding balance higher than I expected?

Your balance is calculated daily and includes any interest that has accrued since your last monthly payment. If a payment was missed, late, or only partially made, additional interest will have been applied. For a full breakdown of charges and a current settlement figure, visit the Settlement Figure page or contact us directly.

How will my payments appear on my bank statement?

Monthly Direct Debit collections will show as AZADI FIN SVCS or AFS on your statement. Card payments made through this portal will appear as AZADI FINANCIAL. If you spot a transaction you don't recognise, please contact us before raising a dispute with your bank so we can investigate promptly.

Can I change the date my monthly payment is collected?

Yes. You're entitled to one payment date change during the lifetime of your agreement. You can do this online through the Change Payment Date page. Please be aware that moving your collection date may result in a slightly longer interval between payments, which can increase the total interest charged over the remaining term.

Why might changing my payment date cost me more?

Interest on your agreement accrues daily. When you move your payment date further into the month — for example from the 1st to the 20th — there are additional days between collections during which interest continues to build. The exact amount depends on your outstanding balance and APR. You'll be asked to confirm you understand this before the change is applied.

Can I settle my agreement early?

Absolutely. You can request a settlement figure at any time through the Settlement Figure page. The figure includes a rebate of future interest charges as required by the Consumer Credit Act 1974. Settlement figures are typically valid for 28 days. Once you've made your payment, we'll confirm the agreement is closed within five working days.

What happens if I miss a payment?

If a payment is missed, we'll contact you to arrange collection as soon as possible. A missed payment may result in additional interest and could affect your credit rating. If you're experiencing financial difficulty, please reach out to our Collections team on 0345 786 1234 as early as you can — we have a range of support options and would far rather work with you than let arrears build up.

How do I update my personal details?

You can update your address, phone number, email, and bank details directly through this portal. Visit My Contact Details for personal information or Update Bank Details to change your Direct Debit account. Changes are applied to all your Azadi Financial Services accounts and may take up to 24 hours to be fully reflected.

I'm thinking of returning my vehicle under Voluntary Termination. How does that work?

Under the Consumer Credit Act 1974, you have the right to voluntarily terminate your Hire Purchase or PCP agreement once you've repaid at least half of the total amount payable. The vehicle must be returned in good condition, reflecting fair wear and tear in line with BVRLA guidelines. To start the process, please contact our customer services team who will explain your options and arrange collection of the vehicle.

Where can I find my agreement number?

Your agreement number appears on the original finance documentation you received from the dealership, in the reference field of your Direct Debit mandate (visible through your online banking), and in the welcome SMS we sent when your agreement began. If you still can't locate it, call us on 0345 786 4321 and we'll look it up using your personal details.
